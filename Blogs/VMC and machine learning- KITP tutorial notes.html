Variational Monte Carlo and machine learning

By Estelle M. Inack

 

Introduction

Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) is a quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) method that has traditionally been used by the condensed matter community to approximate ground-state properties of quantum systems. Routinely, it is implemented as a first step to obtain good approximations of ground state wave-functions which would then be considered as guiding functions for importance sampling in more accurate QMC methods such as diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) or fixed-node quantum Monte Carlo (FNQMC). However, in this tutorial, we will focus on the use of VMC as a stand-alone algorithm and discuss on the different ways it might benefit from machine learning.

Some application of Variational Monte Carlo with artificial neural networks

Different flavours of neural networks have been used to represent ground state wave-functions of quantum many-body systems. The first use of an artificial neural network as a variational Ansatz was made in the seminal paper by Carleo and Troyer, where a restricted Boltzmann machine was utilized to represent the ground-state wave function of spin-based models. Later on Saito used a feed-forward neural network as a variational wavefunction for the Bose-Hubbard model. Other neural architectures such as convolutional neural networks, hybrid neural networks have been used respectively on the frustrated - antiferromagnetic Heisenberg and Hubbard models. Recently, an unrestricted Boltzmann machine was utilized as a guiding wave-function to reduced the computational complexity of projective QMC simulations.

All these works do show that artificial neural networks are indeed powerful to represent complex quantum-many body wave functions. As time unveils, neural networks states will keep revealing their utility as it was shown not long ago in the context of open quantum systems.
